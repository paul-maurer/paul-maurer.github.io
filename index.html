About me

I'm a second year PhD Student in the Team Calisto at Inria Université Côte d'Azur. 

I am working under the supervision of Mireille Bossy on Stochastic models for the dynamics of deformable particles in turbulence. My main interests cover stochastic analysis, numerical probabilities, and random models.

I am also teaching assistant at Université Côte d'Azur, and I am giving oral examinations "colles" for students in engineering preparatory class at Lycée Masséna in Nice. 

Ressearch topics

I am mainly working on Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs) driven by irregular noise. In particular, I'm dealing with jumps-SDEs (for example, driven by Lévy processes), SDEs with fractional Brownian motion, as well as stochastic Volterra equations. Such equations can appear in various application fields, such as turbulence, particle transport, population models, or option pricing models in finance (for example, rough volatility models). They are useful to describe probabilistic phenomenon with a large variety of behavior, including models with heavy tail distributions, anomalous diffusion, and processes with intermittency.
